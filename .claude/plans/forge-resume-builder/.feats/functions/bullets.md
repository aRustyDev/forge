### Patterns

### AI Smell Detection

- **Summary**:
  - feature lists
- **Bullets**:
  - Overly Consistent/Similar Structure
  - Repeated keywords
  - em-dashes
- Formulaic structure
- Buzzword density
- Decision rationale
- Sentence length uniformity
- "forked as a reusable pattern across the organization" — replace with what actually happened
- Remove stacked modifier chains like "ML-ready data ingestion with SQL-accessible lineage tracking"
- Stacked Modifier Chains: phrases where multiple adjectives/qualifiers pile up before the noun, creating density that sounds impressive but is hard to parse.

#### Better Practices

- **Summary**: Write it like a human would — one concrete sentence about what you do, one about your differentiator. Drop the feature list.
- **Bullets**: Vary the structure. Not every bullet needs "Built X using Y; achieved Z." Some should be short. Some should lead with the problem, not the solution.
- First person, conversational, sounds like a human talking to a hiring manager
- Lead with what you actually do (ie 'backend for agents'), not a title
- Human voice markers "nobody could explain", "hitting the ceiling", "within a sprint", "I build the backend systems", "14 years across DoD"
- Names specific companies and contexts (credibility)
- The <domain>/<industry> framing becomes a natural differentiator, not a buzzword
- "mistakes have real consequences" is memorable and authentic
- Write in 'Plain English'
