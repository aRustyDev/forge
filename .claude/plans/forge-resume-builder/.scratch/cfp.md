- **Sessionize**: A premier platform for finding tech conferences and submitting talk proposals.
- **SeeCFP**: An organized Airtable database of upcoming conference calls for proposals (CFPs).
- **LinkedIn/Twitter**: Follow industry experts and search hashtags like #CFP or #CallForPapers to find open slots.
- **Developer Events**: A comprehensive site to find, filter, and apply to various technical events.
- **Local Meetups**: Local conferences and user groups are ideal for first-time speakers and often look for local talent.
- **Specific Communities**: Python conferences are known for being welcoming to new speakers and offering opportunities for "lightning talks".

    - Draft a Strong Proposal: Provide a compelling title, a short public-facing blurb, and a detailed outline for organizers.
    - Start with a Target Topic: Create a concrete idea that offers actionable takeaways for the audience. Use the "Heart-Head-Action" structure to organize your content.
    - Submit Multiple Proposals: Do not rely on just one, as top conferences receive many applicants.
    - Utilize Video Evidence: Include a video of a previous talk (even a small, self-recorded one) to demonstrate your stage presence.
    - Leverage Existing Content: You can repurpose content from blog posts, company presentations, or previous talks, as long as it fits the new conference theme.
